Welcome To Siem Reap

Nestled in northwest Cambodia, Siem Reap is a gateway to the ancient wonders of the Khmer Empire. At its heart lies Angkor Wat, a temple complex spanning 162.6 hectaresâ€”the largest religious monument in the world. Originally built as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu during the reign of King Suryavarman II, it gradually transformed into a Buddhist temple towards the end of the 12th century.

Angkor Wat's design masterfully combines two fundamental plans of Khmer temple architecture: the temple-mountain and the galleried temple. Its impressive structure represents Mount Meru, the sacred home of devas in Hindu mythology, creating a spiritual bridge between heaven and earth.

History Of The Angkor Archaeological Site

The Angkor Wat complex spans approximately 1000 square miles north of Siem Reap, encompassing around 100 temple and monument ruins. Built in the 12th century by Khmer King Suryavarman II as a Hindu temple, the Angkor complex represented the heart of the mighty Khmer Empire, which at its peak extended across Cambodia, Vietnam, and into China.

During the latter half of the 12th Century, Angkor Wat gradually transformed from a Hindu sanctuary to a Buddhist temple, a role it continues to fulfill today. Though Angkor Wat experienced periods of neglect throughout its history, it has always maintained some level of significanceâ€”whether as a destination for Japanese Buddhist pilgrims or simply as a sacred place for local Khmer people.

1. Sunrise at Angkor Wat Temple

On your first morning in Siem Reap, experience the magical moments when golden light bathes the ancient temples of Angkor Wat. The ticket office opens at 4:30 am, allowing you to secure a prime viewing spot before daybreak.

2. Bayon Temple

One of the real highlights of the complex! Bayon was built by King Jayavarman and features 216 smiling face carvings of Avalokiteshvara built into the sides of the 54 towers of the structure.
